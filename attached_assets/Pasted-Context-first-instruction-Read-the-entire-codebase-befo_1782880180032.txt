Core Concept — Full-Width Text Mask Reveal: Each principle is presented as a massive, full-width typographic statement — the title alone fills an entire row at display scale, edge to edge. No columns, no side-by-side layout. Just raw, confident text that commands the screen. Think editorial magazine cover meets technical manifesto.

The Scroll Reveal Mechanism: Each principle title uses a text mask clip animation — the text starts completely hidden behind a clipping mask (appears as if behind a curtain or panel). As the user scrolls and the line enters the viewport, the mask slides away — revealing the text from left to right, like a camera shutter opening or a spotlight sweeping across the words. The reveal should be smooth, confident, and slightly slower than you'd expect — unhurried, like someone making a point.

After the title reveals, the description fades in directly below the title — small, light-weight, a single line or two — with a brief delay. It reads like a footnote to a bold statement.

Typography Treatment:

Principle titles: Enormous. Full container width. Ultra-bold weight. These are not headings — they are declarations. The type should feel like it takes up all the space it deserves.
On alternating principles, the title shifts alignment — first principle left-aligned, second right-aligned, third left, and so on. This creates visual rhythm and prevents the section from feeling monotonous as you scroll.
The number (01, 02, etc.) sits as a tiny superscript or inline prefix before the title — barely visible, a ghost reference, not a feature.
The Accent Word Treatment: Within each principle title, identify the single most powerful word (e.g., "Simplicity" in "Simplicity is a feature") and render it in the accent color. Just that one word. Everything else is white/default. This creates a focal point in each statement and makes the section feel curated and intentional.

Hover Interaction: When the user hovers over any principle:

The accent-colored word in that principle glows brighter and subtly scales up (1.02–1.05x) — barely perceptible but felt.
A thin underline in the accent color draws itself under the entire title from left to right.
The description below shifts up slightly and becomes fully opaque if it was partially muted.
All other principles dim to about 30% opacity — bringing full focus to the hovered one.
Between Each Principle: A razor-thin horizontal divider — but instead of a static line, it pulses once with a brief glow in the accent color when the principle above it finishes its reveal animation. Then it settles to a static, barely-visible rule. Like punctuation at the end of a sentence.

Section Header: "What I believe." should feel different from the rest of the site's section headers. Each word reveals itself with a vertical wipe — words appear from bottom to top, one word at a time, like they're rising into view. The period at the end arrives last, with a tiny pause — like a full stop being deliberately placed.

Feel and Tone: This should feel like reading someone's code of ethics — each statement is a law, a conviction, a line in the sand. The bold type, the confident reveals, the accent word — all of it communicates that these aren't preferences, they're principles. The pacing should be slow and deliberate throughout.
